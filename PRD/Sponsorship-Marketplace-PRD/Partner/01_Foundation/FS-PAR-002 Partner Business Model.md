# 1. Introduction

## Purpose

This document defines the complete business model for the Partner ecosystem within the Event Sponsorship Marketplace.

It explains:

- Why Partners exist
- How they create value
- How they generate revenue
- How they interact with Sponsors and Organizers
- How the platform supports multiple partner business models

This document serves as the foundation for all Partner Workspace features and workflows.

---

# 2. Business Vision

To build a scalable partner ecosystem that connects sponsors and organizers through trusted intermediaries, increasing sponsorship success while creating sustainable revenue opportunities for partners.

---

# 3. Business Objectives

The Partner program aims to:

- Expand marketplace reach
- Increase sponsorship conversions
- Improve sponsor acquisition
- Improve organizer acquisition
- Provide expert sponsorship consulting
- Reduce deal closure time
- Increase campaign success
- Improve client retention
- Generate recurring business
- Build long-term strategic partnerships

# 5. Partner Definition

A Partner is an individual or organization authorized to represent clients, identify sponsorship opportunities, negotiate agreements, coordinate campaigns, and earn commissions through successful sponsorship transactions.

Partners do not own sponsorship inventory or event assets. They create value through relationships, expertise, and business development.

---

# 6. Partner Value Proposition

Partners deliver value by:

- Acquiring new sponsors and organizers
- Building trusted relationships
- Identifying suitable sponsorship opportunities
- Providing strategic sponsorship advice
- Matching sponsors with relevant events
- Supporting negotiations
- Coordinating campaign execution
- Improving campaign outcomes
- Driving repeat business

# 7. Business Model

## Primary Business Activities

### Client Acquisition

Partners acquire sponsors and organizers through:

- Direct outreach
- Referrals
- Marketplace leads
- Networking
- Existing relationships
- Marketing campaigns

---

### Relationship Management

Partners maintain ongoing relationships through:

- Regular meetings
- Quarterly reviews
- Campaign planning
- Performance reporting
- Renewal discussions

---

### Opportunity Discovery

Partners search the marketplace to identify opportunities aligned with client objectives.

Evaluation criteria include:

- Industry
- Audience
- Budget
- Geography
- Brand alignment
- Campaign goals
- Historical performance

---

### Opportunity Matching

Partners recommend opportunities using:

- Manual search
- AI recommendations
- Historical success
- Client preferences

---

### Negotiation

Partners coordinate discussions between Sponsors and Organizers to reach mutually beneficial agreements.

---

### Campaign Coordination

After agreement, Partners monitor campaign execution by:

- Tracking deliverables
- Monitoring timelines
- Coordinating communication
- Resolving issues
- Reporting progress

---

### Relationship Expansion

Partners identify opportunities for:

- Upselling
- Cross-selling
- Renewals
- Multi-event agreements
- Long-term partnerships

# 8. Revenue Model

Partners may earn revenue through multiple models.

## Percentage Commission

A percentage of sponsorship value.

Example:

```
Deal Value

↓

Commission %

↓

Commission Amount
```

---

## Fixed Fee

A predefined fee for facilitating a sponsorship.

---

## Consulting Fee

Charged for strategic sponsorship advisory services.

---

## Campaign Management Fee

Recurring fee for managing sponsorship execution.

---

## Retainer Model

Monthly or annual service agreements.

---

## Hybrid Model

Combination of:

- Fixed fee
- Commission
- Performance bonus

# 17. Business Rules

## Client Ownership

A client may only have one primary partner unless explicitly configured for shared ownership.

---

## Opportunity Assignment

Each opportunity must be assigned to a responsible partner.

---

## Deal Ownership

Deals inherit ownership from the associated opportunity.

---

## Commission Eligibility

Commission is generated only after:

- Contract execution
- Payment confirmation
- Commission approval

---

## Renewal Ownership

Existing partner retains renewal rights unless reassigned.

---

## Audit Requirement

Every critical business action must be recorded in the audit log.
